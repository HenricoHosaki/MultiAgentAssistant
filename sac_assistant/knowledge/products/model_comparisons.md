# Model Comparisons

## Keyboards: BlackWidow V4 X vs Kumara K552 vs K70 RGB Pro
The Corsair K70 RGB Pro is the premium option, with the highest polling rate (8000 Hz),
full per-key RGB, and an optical-mechanical switch built for the fastest response. The
Razer BlackWidow V4 X sits in the middle, with solid build quality and a 1000 Hz polling
rate, but without the K70's ultra-high polling rate. The Redragon Kumara K552 is the
budget pick: it lacks N-key rollover, RGB customization, and has a much lower polling rate
(125 Hz), but costs a fraction of the other two.

## Mice: G502 X Plus vs DeathAdder V3 vs M711 Cobra
The Logitech G502 X Plus is the only wireless option of the three and has the highest
button count (13 programmable buttons), making it best for users who want onboard macros
and freedom from a cable. The Razer DeathAdder V3 is the lightest (59g) and has the
highest max DPI (30,000), making it the strongest pick for competitive, fast-flick
gameplay. The Redragon M711 Cobra is the most affordable, with decent specs for casual
play but a noticeably lower max DPI and fewer programmable buttons than the other two.

## Headsets: BlackShark V2 Pro vs Cloud II vs HS65 Surround
The Razer BlackShark V2 Pro is the only wireless model and has the best microphone quality
(HyperClear cardioid), making it the top pick for streaming or professional use, at the
highest price point. The HyperX Cloud II offers the best balance of comfort and surround
sound features for the price, with a detachable noise-cancelling microphone. The Corsair
HS65 Surround is the lightest and most affordable of the three, but its microphone is
omnidirectional rather than noise-cancelling, making it less ideal for noisy environments.

## Monitors: LG UltraGear 27GP850 vs Samsung Odyssey G5
The LG UltraGear 27GP850 has a higher refresh rate (165Hz vs 144Hz) and is the only one
certified for NVIDIA G-SYNC, making it the better pick for NVIDIA GPU owners. The Samsung
Odyssey G5 has a 1000R curved panel, which some users find more comfortable for long
sessions, and costs less, but tops out at 144Hz and lacks G-SYNC certification.

## Gaming Chairs: DXRacer Formula Series vs ThunderX3 EC3
The DXRacer Formula Series has a classic racing-style design with PU leather and a lower
weight capacity (100kg), suited to users who prefer a structured, firm seat. The
ThunderX3 EC3 has a higher weight capacity (130kg), a deeper recline range, and a
breathable mesh back, making it the better choice for taller/heavier users or hot
climates, at a lower price point.
