## Compatibility — Mice
- All three mice work as plug-and-play USB HID devices on Windows, macOS, and Linux.
- Button remapping and DPI profiles require manufacturer software (Logitech G HUB or Razer
  Synapse), available only for Windows and macOS. The Redragon M711 uses its own lightweight
  configuration tool, Windows only.
- The G502 X Plus is the only model in this list with wireless connectivity; the other two
  are wired only.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Mice
- Q: Can I use the G502 X Plus wired instead of wireless?
  A: Yes, it can be connected via USB cable for charging and zero-latency wired play, while
  still functioning as a regular wired mouse without the LIGHTSPEED receiver.
- Q: Is the Redragon M711 suitable for competitive gaming?
  A: It is a budget option and performs well for casual and semi-competitive play, but its
  sensor and polling rate are not on par with the DeathAdder V3 or G502 X Plus for
  high-level esports use.
- Q: Do these mice support on-the-fly DPI switching without software?
  A: Yes, all three have a physical DPI button that cycles through preset DPI stages stored
  in onboard memory, even without the configuration software installed.
