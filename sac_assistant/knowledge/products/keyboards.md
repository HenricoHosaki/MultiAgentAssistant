## Compatibility — Keyboards
- All three models work on Windows 10/11 without additional drivers for basic typing.
- RGB lighting customization (BlackWidow V4 X and K70 RGB Pro) requires the manufacturer's
  software (Razer Synapse or iCUE), available for Windows and macOS.
- The Kumara K552 has fixed backlight only and does not require any software.
- None of these keyboards have native console (PlayStation/Xbox) support; they are designed
  for PC use.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Keyboards
- Q: Can I use these keyboards on macOS?
  A: Yes, all three work as standard USB keyboards on macOS. RGB customization software is
  only available for the BlackWidow V4 X and K70 RGB Pro, and key positions follow the
  Windows layout (no native Mac key legends).
- Q: What's the difference between the switch types?
  A: Razer Green/Yellow and Corsair OPX are tactile/linear mechanical switches built for
  gaming, with shorter actuation distances. The Outemu Blue switches on the Kumara K552 are
  clicky and louder, closer to a traditional mechanical typing feel.
- Q: Are replacement keycaps available?
  A: Keycaps for the K70 RGB Pro and BlackWidow V4 X follow standard Cherry MX-compatible
  stems and accept most aftermarket keycap sets. The Kumara K552 uses Outemu-compatible stems,
  which have a smaller aftermarket selection.
