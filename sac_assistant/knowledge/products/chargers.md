- Output: 65W total (single port), supports USB Power Delivery 3.0
- Ports: 1x USB-C
- Technology: GaN (gallium nitride), smaller and cooler-running than traditional chargers
- Cable included: No (cable sold separately)

- Capacity: 20,000 mAh
- Output: 2x USB-A (12W each), 1x USB-C (18W, Power Delivery)
- Input: USB-C, 18W (recharging the power bank itself)

- Compatible devices: Phones, tablets, some USB-C laptops (low-power models only)

## Compatibility — Chargers and Power
- The Anker 65W GaNPrime charger can charge most USB-C laptops, but laptops requiring more
  than 65W (typical for high-performance gaming laptops) will charge slowly or not at all
  under load.
- The PowerCore 20000mAh's 18W USB-C output is enough for phones, tablets, and very
  low-power USB-C laptops, but is not sufficient for gaming laptops or most ultrabooks
  with higher power demands.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Chargers and Power
- Q: Can the Anker 65W charger replace my gaming laptop's original charger?
  A: Only if your gaming laptop's original charger is rated at 65W or less. Most gaming
  laptops require 100W–240W and will not charge properly, or at all, with this charger.
- Q: Can I use the power bank to charge a laptop?
  A: Only laptops that charge over USB-C at 18W or less, such as some ultra-low-power
  ultrabooks. Most laptops, especially gaming laptops, require more power than this power
  bank can provide via USB-C.
- Q: Is the GaN charger safe to leave plugged in permanently?
  A: Yes, GaN chargers include the same safety protections (overcurrent, overheat, short
  circuit) as traditional chargers, and are designed to run cooler due to the GaN material's
  higher efficiency.
