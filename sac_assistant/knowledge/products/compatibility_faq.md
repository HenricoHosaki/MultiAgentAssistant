# General Compatibility FAQ

## Cross-Category Platform Support
Most peripherals in our catalog (keyboards, mice, headsets, webcams, mousepads) are
plug-and-play USB HID or UVC devices and work on Windows, macOS, and Linux without extra
drivers for basic functionality. Advanced features — RGB customization, button remapping,
DPI profiles, or surround sound — typically require manufacturer software, which is usually
available only for Windows and macOS, not Linux.

## Console Compatibility Overview
- Headsets with a 3.5mm connection work on PlayStation and Xbox consoles for basic audio.
- Wireless dongles for headsets and mice (2.4 GHz proprietary dongles) are PC-only and do
  not pair with consoles.
- Controllers are platform-specific: the Xbox Wireless Controller is not officially
  supported on PlayStation, and vice versa for PlayStation controllers (not currently sold
  in our catalog).
- Monitors accept any HDMI source, including consoles, but refresh rates above 120Hz
  require a PC; current-generation consoles are limited to 120Hz over HDMI.

## Frequently Asked Questions — General Compatibility
- Q: If a product doesn't mention macOS or Linux, does that mean it doesn't work at all?
  A: No. Basic functionality (typing, clicking, audio output) works on any OS for USB HID
  devices. The absence of macOS/Linux support usually refers only to the configuration
  software (RGB, macros, DPI profiles), not basic device function.
- Q: Can I mix and match brands (e.g., a Razer mouse with Corsair software)?
  A: No, each manufacturer's configuration software (Razer Synapse, Corsair iCUE, Logitech
  G HUB) only recognizes and configures that brand's own devices.
- Q: Do USB-C peripherals work with USB-A ports using an adapter?
  A: Generally yes, for data and basic charging, using a simple USB-C-to-USB-A adapter.
  However, high-power devices (like USB-C docking stations with high wattage power delivery)
  may not function at full capability through a basic adapter.
