# Monitors Catalog

## Gaming Monitor [LG UltraGear 27GP850]
A 27-inch QHD gaming monitor with a fast refresh rate and Nano IPS panel, built for competitive gaming with minimal motion blur.

- Panel type: Nano IPS
- Resolution: 2560x1440 (QHD)
- Refresh rate: 165 Hz (overclockable to 180 Hz)
- Response time: 1 ms (GtG with motion blur reduction)
- Adaptive sync: NVIDIA G-SYNC Compatible, AMD FreeSync Premium
- Ports: 2x HDMI 2.0, 1x DisplayPort 1.4
- VESA mount compatible: Yes (100x100mm)
- Price: USD $379.99

## Gaming Monitor [Samsung Odyssey G5]
A curved 27-inch QHD monitor with a 1000R curvature designed to match the natural curve of the human eye, reducing visual fatigue during long sessions.

- Panel type: VA (curved, 1000R)
- Resolution: 2560x1440 (QHD)
- Refresh rate: 144 Hz
- Response time: 1 ms (MPRT)
- Adaptive sync: AMD FreeSync Premium
- Ports: 1x HDMI 2.0, 1x DisplayPort 1.2
- VESA mount compatible: Yes (100x100mm)
- Price: USD $279.99

## Compatibility — Monitors
- Both monitors support adaptive sync technologies, but only the LG UltraGear 27GP850 is
  officially certified as NVIDIA G-SYNC Compatible; the Samsung Odyssey G5 supports FreeSync
  only and is not G-SYNC certified, though it may work with NVIDIA cards via FreeSync
  compatibility on some driver versions.
- Both monitors are VESA mount compatible (100x100mm pattern) and can be used with standard
  monitor arms and mounts.
- Console use (PS5/Xbox Series X) is supported on both via HDMI, but 144Hz/165Hz refresh
  rates are limited by the console's HDMI version and output settings, not the monitor.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Monitors
- Q: Which monitor is better for a curved desk setup?
  A: The Samsung Odyssey G5 has a 1000R curve designed to reduce eye strain on wide views;
  the LG UltraGear 27GP850 is flat. Choice depends on personal preference for curved vs flat
  displays.
- Q: Do these monitors include speakers?
  A: No, neither model has built-in speakers. An external speaker or headset is required.
- Q: Can I run both at 1440p 165Hz on a console?
  A: No. Current consoles (PS5/Xbox Series X) output a maximum of 1440p at 120Hz over HDMI;
  the full 165Hz/144Hz refresh rate is only achievable on PC.
