# Controllers Catalog

## Wireless Controller [Xbox Wireless Controller]
The standard wireless controller for Xbox consoles and Windows PCs, featuring a hybrid D-pad and textured grip for improved comfort.

- Connectivity: Bluetooth, Xbox Wireless (with adapter), USB-C wired
- Battery: 2x AA batteries (or optional rechargeable battery pack, sold separately)
- Compatible platforms: Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One, Windows 10/11
- Vibration feedback: Yes (standard rumble motors)
- Audio jack: 3.5mm headset jack built in
- Available colors: Black, White, Robot White
- Price: USD $59.99

## Wireless Controller [8BitDo Ultimate Controller]
A multi-platform wireless controller with hall-effect joysticks for improved durability, designed to work across PC, Switch, and Android.

- Connectivity: 2.4 GHz wireless dongle, Bluetooth
- Battery: Built-in rechargeable, up to 22 hours
- Compatible platforms: Windows 10/11, Nintendo Switch, Android, Raspberry Pi
- Vibration feedback: Yes (standard rumble motors)
- Audio jack: None (audio via connected device only)
- Available colors: Black, White
- Price: USD $64.99

## Compatibility — Controllers
- The Xbox Wireless Controller works natively on Xbox consoles and Windows PCs, but requires
  a third-party adapter or software for use on Nintendo Switch or most Android devices.
- The 8BitDo Ultimate Controller is the more universal option, with official support across
  Windows, Switch, and Android, but it is not officially licensed for Xbox or PlayStation
  consoles.
- Neither controller is officially compatible with PlayStation consoles.

## Frequently Asked Questions — Controllers
- Q: Can I use the Xbox Wireless Controller on a PlayStation 5?
  A: No, it is not officially supported on PlayStation consoles. It works on Xbox consoles
  and Windows PCs only.
- Q: Does the 8BitDo Ultimate Controller need batteries?
  A: No, it has a built-in rechargeable battery (up to 22 hours) charged via USB-C, unlike
  the Xbox Wireless Controller, which uses AA batteries by default.
- Q: What does "hall-effect joystick" mean, and why does it matter?
  A: Hall-effect joysticks use magnetic sensors instead of physical contact points, which
  significantly reduces the risk of stick drift over time compared to traditional
  potentiometer-based joysticks.
